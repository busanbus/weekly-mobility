Key Initiatives: Report to the President

In its presidential policy report for the second half of 2026, the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport announced four major reforms (comprehensive reform of national land use; structural reform of land, infrastructure, and transportation services; and theRectification of Illegal and Loopholed Practices, and Reliable Administration That Brings About Change in Everyday Life) and four strategic priorities (Housing Stability, Inclusive Growth, Transportation Innovation, and Future Growth).

National Assembly

Calculation of Cost Savings from Raising the Age Eligibility for Senior Discounts on Urban and Conventional Rail Systems to 70

According to the National Assembly Budget Office, it is estimated that raising the age eligibility for senior citizen fare discounts on urban and commuter rail systems nationwide from 65 to 70 would result in a total reduction of approximately 3.5 trillion won in fare discounts over a five-year period (2027–2031).

South Gyeongsang Province

Briefing on Transportation Policy for the 9th Popularly Elected Administration: Introduction of the Gyeongnam-Style Regional Express Bus

At a briefing on transportation policy for the 9th popularly elected administration, South Gyeongsang Province announced that it would introduce regional express bus routes connecting major hubs within the province, as well as between South Gyeongsang and Busan and between South Gyeongsang and Ulsan, enabling travel from anywhere in the province to these major hubs within about an hour.

Society

Characteristics of Private Car Ownership by Age Group (as of June 2026)

According to the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport’s vehicle registration statistics, as of the end of June 2026, vehicles registered under the names of owners with 50 or more vehicles accounted for 50.2% of all privately registered vehicles in the country, exceeding the 50% mark for the first time since records began.

Busan

Four Districts in Phase 2 of the Aging Planned Cities Project; Draft Master Plan for the Renovation of Aging Planned Cities Prepared

Following the first phase—Hwamyeong, Geumgok, and Haeundae—Busan Metropolitan City has prepared a draft master plan for the redevelopment of aging planned cities in the four districts designated for the second phase—Gaegum, Danggam, Mandeok, and Dadae—and has begun the public review process.
