Ministry of Land, Infrastructure, and Transport

Payment of Diesel Fuel Subsidies for Chartered Buses Begins

The Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport has approved a draft amendment to the enforcement decree to include charter buses in the scope of the diesel fuel subsidy program, and plans to revise the payment guidelines in July to begin disbursing the subsidies.

Seoul

Announcement: “Changes to Life in Seoul in the Second Half of 2026”

The Seoul Metropolitan Government announced 60 initiatives across seven areas—including regulatory reform, welfare and health, and traffic safety—as part of its “Changes to Life in Seoul in the Second Half of 2026” plan, and stated that, in the transportation sector, the time period during which the basic fare for community buses applies will be extended from one hour to two hours.

Daejeon

Introduction of 3-section articulated buses at risk of being scrapped

Reports indicate that while Daejeon City began a pilot operation of one three-section articulated bus—which it had planned to introduce in 2025—in April 2026, delivery of the remaining two buses has been delayed because the company with which the city signed the import contract is on the verge of bankruptcy, making it increasingly likely that the contract will be terminated.

Jeju

Pushing for a Review of the Project to Introduce Island-Style Bus Stops and Double-Door Buses

It was reported that Jeju Province has decided to conduct a comprehensive review of the island-style bus stops and double-door buses it has been promoting since 2024, under the 9th popularly elected administration.
